The Problems We're Fixing

You've Accepted These as Normal.
They Aren't.

The internet was built for engineers and handed to everyone else. These are the daily frustrations that hundreds of millions of people have just stopped noticing.

// PROBLEM 01
You Have 300 Passwords and You're One Breach Away From Losing Everything
The average person manages 300+ passwords. They reuse them because they have no choice. Every week brings another breach, another reset email, another hour lost on something that should not exist. The web forces you to maintain a secret identity just to use it.
// PROBLEM 02
You're Paying for 12 Platforms and Fully Using Maybe 2
Website builder. Email tool. Social scheduler. Design app. Note-taker. Scheduling tool. Each with its own login, learning curve, and monthly bill demanding your attention. The average household spends $273/month on subscriptions and can't name half of them.
// PROBLEM 03
Publishing One Blog Post Requires Six Different Apps
Write in Google Docs. Copy to WordPress. Find an image in Canva. Upload it. Add SEO. Schedule in Buffer. Share in Hootsuite. Six apps, six logins, forty-five minutes — for one post that took ten minutes to write. The infrastructure has become more work than the actual thing.
// PROBLEM 04
Your Ideas Disappear Before You Can Do Anything With Them
Voice memos nobody transcribes. Notes in apps you forgot you had. Emails to yourself. Ideas you were sure you'd remember. The friction between having a thought and capturing it is just high enough that most great ideas never make it anywhere. They evaporate.
// PROBLEM 05
Building a Website Still Requires Either Money, Time, or a Nephew Who "Knows Computers"
The internet has been around for 30 years. Having a web presence still requires hiring a designer, learning a platform, or calling in a favor. Most small businesses, artists, and everyday people either have no website or have one that embarrasses them. This is not a skill problem. It is a tool problem.
